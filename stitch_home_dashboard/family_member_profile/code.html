The Life Story

Born in the heart of the Deep South during a pivotal decade, Eleanor grew up in the shade of ancient oaks and the fragrance of blooming jasmine. Her early years were defined by a profound curiosity for the natural world, often found sketching local flora in her father's garden—a passion that would later define her career in botanical illustration.

She moved to the Pacific Northwest in the late 60s, seeking a change in scenery that mirrored her inner transformation. It was there she met Arthur, beginning a fifty-year partnership built on shared values of community, art, and the preservation of family stories. Her legacy is one of quiet strength and vivid color.
